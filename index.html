Financial Management Course

An in-depth exploration of core financial management principles and practices.

Course Description

The course on “Financial Management” provides an in-depth exploration of the core principles and practices of financial management. It equips students with essential knowledge to make sound financial decisions for businesses and organisations, covering fundamental topics such as capital budgeting, investment analysis, working capital management, and dividend policies. Participants will learn to assess finance from different sources, evaluate investment opportunities, allocate financial resources effectively, and strategize for long-term growth in alignment with both corporate goals and shareholder interests.

Learning Objectives

  • Introduce the basic concept of financial management.
  • Understand the various factors influencing financial management.
  • Acquaint students with different stages of financial management in corporate sectors.

Target Audience

This course is ideal for aspiring financial managers, business students, or professionals looking to enhance their financial knowledge.

Course Modules

Module 1: Concepts of Financial Management

Introduction to Financial Management, Nature and Scope, Objectives, Time Value of Money. Duration: 4 Hours

Module 2: Investment Analysis - Capital Budgeting

Meaning and Nature of Capital Budgeting, Needs, and Importance, Capital Budgeting Process, Techniques of Capital Budgeting. Duration: 5 Hours

Module 3: Financing Decisions - Capital Structure

Meaning of Capital Structure, Factors Determining Capital Structure, Capital Structure Theories, Cost of Capital, Financial Leverage, Operating Leverage. Duration: 6 Hours

Module 4: Working Capital Management

Meaning, Nature, and Objectives of Working Capital Management, Determinants of Working Capital, Determination of Working Capital Requirement. Duration: 5 Hours
